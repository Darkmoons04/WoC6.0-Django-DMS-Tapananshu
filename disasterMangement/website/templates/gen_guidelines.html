Disaster Safety Measures

1. Prevention

This involves taking proactive measures to prevent the occurrence of disasters. This could include proper urban planning, adherence to safety codes, and environmental conservation.

2. Mitigation

This involves reducing the risk of any disaster or its severity or consequences. This could involve building flood barriers, earthquake-resistant structures, and implementing fire safety measures.

3. Capacity Building

This includes research and knowledge management to understand disasters better and develop effective strategies to deal with them.

4. Preparedness

This involves being ready to deal with any disaster. This could include having evacuation plans in place, training emergency response teams, and educating the public about what to do in a disaster.

5. Response

This involves taking immediate action when a disaster occurs. This could include rescue operations, providing immediate medical care, and ensuring the provision of food, water, and shelter.

6. Rehabilitation and Reconstruction

After a disaster, it's important to help affected people return to normal life and to rebuild damaged infrastructure.

In addition to these, specific guidelines exist for different types of disasters such as earthquakes, floods, cyclones, etc. It's important to be aware of the guidelines relevant to the disasters common in your area.
